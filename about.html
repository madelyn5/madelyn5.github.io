I am an undergraduate at the University of Utah majoring in mathematics and minoring in computer science and biology. I expect to graduate in May 2027. Upon the completion of my undergraduate degree, I plan to pursue a PhD in computer science or mathematics and continue with research in academia.

My research interests include graph theory, computational complexity, parameterized algorithms, and bioinformatics. I am particularly interested in working on combinatorial problems and algorithm development movtivated by biological applications.

I've been involved in research since August 2024 when I joined Dr. Blair Sullivan's Theory in Practice group at the University of Utah. Since then, I have been involved in a variety of projects: computationally validating the performance of heuristics for fair information distribution, developing a fixed-parameter tractable (FPT) algorithm for graph modification with a fairness objective, and proving NP-hardness for the same problem. In the summer of 2025, I participated in a Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) in Bozeman, MT, where I contributed to the development of a pipeline for predicting accessible chromatin regions (ACRs) in plant genomes using machine learning and co-linear chaining of cis-regulatory elements (CREs). I recently presented this work at BICOB-2026 in Honolulu, Hawaii.

I am currently collaborating with ENS de Lyon researchers on partitioning graphs into induced subgraphs with specified degeneracies. Additionally, I am involved in a project with other researchers at the University of Utah investigating the optimization of paths for cross-stitching.
